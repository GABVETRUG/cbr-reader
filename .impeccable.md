## Design Context

### Users
Comic book readers accessing their personal CBR collection from a mobile phone on a local network. They want a relaxed, distraction-free reading experience — like settling into a couch with a stack of comics. The primary device is a smartphone held in portrait or landscape, used casually at home.

### Brand Personality
**Immersive. Effortless. Cinematic.**

The interface should evoke the feeling of a personal, premium comic book reader — like a high-end app you'd pay for, but free and self-hosted. The emotional goal is *focus and flow*: the reader disappears into the story, and the UI gets out of the way.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Dark & immersive. Deep blacks and charcoal grays as the canvas, letting comic art pop with full vibrancy.
- **UI Philosophy:** Minimal chrome. Controls fade away during reading. The comic page IS the interface.
- **Typography:** Clean, modern sans-serif. Subtle and lightweight — never competing with the comic's own lettering.
- **Color Accents:** A single warm accent color (amber/gold) for interactive elements, progress indicators, and highlights — evoking the warmth of a reading lamp.
- **Surfaces:** Subtle depth with soft shadows. Cover thumbnails float slightly above the dark background.
- **Animations:** Smooth, physics-based page transitions. Gentle fade-ins. Nothing jarring or cartoonish.
- **Anti-references:** Do NOT look like a file manager or a generic file browser. Avoid flat gray enterprise UI. Avoid cluttered toolbars. Avoid bright white backgrounds that strain eyes during long reading sessions.
- **References:** Apple Books dark mode, Panels app (iOS), Chunky Reader — polished, focused reading experiences.

### Design Principles

1. **Content is King** — The comic page dominates the viewport. Every pixel of UI chrome must earn its place. Hide what isn't actively needed.

2. **Touch-First, Always** — Every interaction is designed for thumbs on glass. Generous tap targets, natural gestures (swipe, pinch, tap), no hover-dependent interactions.

3. **Seamless Flow** — Transitions between pages, between comics, between library and reader should feel continuous and fluid. No jarring jumps or loading walls.

4. **Quiet Until Needed** — Navigation, progress, and controls reveal themselves on tap and fade away on their own. The default state is pure content.

5. **Warm & Inviting** — Despite the dark theme, the experience should feel cozy and personal, not cold or technical. Warm accents, soft edges, welcoming empty states.

### Technical Stack
- **Backend:** Node.js + Express — serves CBR files, extracts images, provides REST API
- **Frontend:** Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS or lightweight framework — mobile-first responsive design
- **Navigation:** Dual mode — horizontal swipe (comic-style) and vertical scroll (webtoon-style), user-switchable
- **Features:** Cover art library grid, reading progress/bookmarks, pinch-to-zoom with pan
